Yewon Kwon

Hello! I study the politics of international conflict and coercion — how states bargain under threat, leverage economic tools to promote their strategic interests. My work draws on quantitative methods and formal theory to understand when coercion works and when it fails.

Research Interests

🛡️

International Security & Conflict Bargaining

Examining how states signal resolve, manage crises, and reach (or fail to reach) negotiated settlements in militarized disputes.

⚖️

Economic Statecraft & Sanctions

Analyzing when and how economic coercion — sanctions, trade restrictions, financial warfare — succeeds as a tool of foreign policy.

☢️

Coercive Diplomacy & Nuclear Politics

Investigating the strategic logic of nuclear deterrence, compellence, and the conditions under which nuclear threats shape state behavior.
